About

AI agents you can actually trust.

PrivateForge is a small, opinionated team building the agent platform we wished existed when we tried to roll out Cursor at our last companies.

The story

In late 2024, every senior engineer I knew was secretly using Cursor or Claude Code on their work laptop — and quietly turning it off the moment they touched anything sensitive. The capability was real. The data path was wrong.

The "obvious" answer was an enterprise SaaS plan with a "we don't train on your data" clause. But the architecture was unchanged: every byte of source still flowed through someone else's servers. The risk surface didn't shrink. It just moved into a contract.

PrivateForge is the architecture flipped: pull the agent to the data, not the data to the agent. Same capability. Different trust boundary.

Mission

Make agentic AI safe for the work that actually matters. The work where you can't ship the codebase up. The work where your security team has a real opinion. The work that pays the bills.

Values

Architecture over marketing.

"We don't train on your data" is a promise. Running on your machine is a property of the system. We pick the system every time.

Boring on purpose.

The bar isn't novelty — it's reliability. We use proven building blocks (KMS, Browserbase, E2B) and stay out of the way.

Honest pricing.

Flat monthly fee. No tokens. No usage tiers we expect you to overshoot. You bring the LLM keys; pay providers what they cost.

Open by default.

The agent's protocol, its CLI, and its evals are public. If we ever get bought, you can keep using the binary you have.

Founder note

"I built the tool I wanted on my own laptop."

I spent years inside high-trust codebases — fintech, healthcare, infrastructure — where the answer to "can we use Cursor?" was always the same dance: a long meeting with security, a half-approved compromise, and a quiet retreat to ChatGPT-after-hours.

PrivateForge is the version where the meeting is short and the answer is yes. Same agent power my friends use on side projects. Same data path my employer's security team can sign off on. No compromise on either end.

If you're building something serious and you've felt this exact friction — I'd love to hear from you.

— Kunal Patel, Founder
