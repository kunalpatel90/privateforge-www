Security & privacy

The trust boundary runs through your hardware.

PrivateForge was built for teams who can't ship their codebase to a SaaS agent. That's not a marketing claim — it's the architecture.

The privacy model in one diagram

                ┌──────────────────────────────────────────┐
                │   YOUR LAPTOP / VPC / OFFICE SERVER       │
                │  ┌────────────────────────────────────┐  │
                │  │  PrivateForge agent (binary)       │  │
                │  │  ├─ filesystem · shell · memory    │  │
                │  │  ├─ browser (Browserbase)          │  │
                │  │  └─ sandbox (E2B)                  │  │
                │  └────────────┬───────────────────────┘  │
                └───────────────┼──────────────────────────┘
                                │  direct, your key, no proxy
                                ▼
                       ┌─────────────────┐
                       │   Anthropic /   │
                       │   OpenAI /      │
                       │   Google APIs   │
                       └─────────────────┘

PrivateForge's cloud handles auth, billing, and the org directory. It never sees your prompts, code, or LLM responses.

BYOK · no LLM proxy

You bring API keys for Anthropic, OpenAI, and/or Google. The agent calls those providers directly from your machine. We never see prompts, completions, or tool outputs.

KMS envelope encryption

Every secret — provider keys, OAuth tokens, deploy creds — is encrypted at rest using AWS KMS envelope encryption. Each secret has its own data key, wrapped by your customer master key.

Self-hosted by default

The agent runs as a binary on your machine, your VPC, or your office server. Data plane and control plane are physically separate.

Auditable history

Every action the agent takes — files read, commands run, calls made — is recorded in a structured history that you can query, export, and ship to your SIEM.

What we don't do

The list of things PrivateForge will never do.

A clear "no" list is more useful than a vague "yes" list. Here's what we explicitly don't, and won't, do.

  • We don't proxy LLM calls. Your provider keys hit the provider directly from your machine.
  • We don't train on your data. No model fine-tuning. No telemetry of prompts. No "anonymized samples."
  • We don't read your code. Source files never leave your filesystem unless you point a tool at one that ships them somewhere (e.g., your own deploy command).
  • We don't store prompts or completions on our servers. History lives locally with the agent.
  • We don't decrypt your KMS-protected secrets server-side. Decryption happens on the agent runtime, after the agent has authenticated to your KMS as your IAM principal.
  • We don't share your usage with model providers. Provider keys are yours; their billing reports stay on their dashboards.
  • We don't ship a "cloud-hosted" version of the agent runtime. The agent is, by definition, the part you control.
Compliance roadmap

What's on our compliance roadmap.

We're a pre-SOC2 company building toward formal compliance. Here's what's in flight:

  • SOC 2 Type I — initial audit window opens Q3 2026.
  • SOC 2 Type II — target Q1 2027 (after 6-month observation period).
  • SSO (SAML, OIDC) — engineering complete, beta Q3 2026.
  • HIPAA-eligible deployment guide — for self-hosted installs in customer-controlled VPCs.
  • SBOM and SLSA provenance for every release of the agent binary.

If you have a specific compliance requirement, write us at hello@privateforge.ai. The architecture is the answer to most of them — we just have to document it for your auditor.
